The revenue visualization feature provides a clear view of your business performance over time. Monitor monthly revenue trends to understand your business growth and identify patterns.

## Revenue overview

Revenue data in nexDash represents your monthly income, calculated from paid invoices. The revenue chart displays the last 12 months of data to help you track business performance.

### Revenue data structure

```typescript
type Revenue = {
  month: string;  // Month name (e.g., "Jan", "Feb")
  revenue: number;  // Revenue amount for the month
}
```

<Note>
  Revenue data is aggregated from all invoices marked as "paid" during each month.
</Note>

## Revenue chart

The revenue chart appears on your main dashboard, providing a visual representation of your monthly income.

### Chart features

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Visual elements">
    The revenue chart includes:
    - **Bar chart** - Each bar represents one month's revenue
    - **Y-axis labels** - Show revenue amounts in USD
    - **X-axis labels** - Display month names
    - **Height scaling** - Bars scale proportionally to revenue amounts
    - **12-month view** - Displays the last year of revenue data
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Interactive elements">
    - Hover over bars to see exact amounts
    - Responsive design adapts to screen size
    - Mobile-friendly vertical layout
    - Calendar icon indicator showing time period
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Chart dimensions

The revenue chart uses the following specifications:

```typescript
const chartHeight = 350;  // pixels
```

Bar heights are calculated proportionally:

```typescript
barHeight = (chartHeight / topLabel) * month.revenue
```

## Understanding revenue metrics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How is revenue calculated?">
    Revenue is the sum of all invoices with a `paid` status for each month. Only completed payments contribute to revenue figures.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What time period is shown?">
    The chart displays the last 12 months of revenue data, giving you a full year view of business performance at a glance.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="When does revenue update?">
    Revenue data updates when:
    - An invoice is marked as paid
    - An invoice status changes from paid to pending (revenue decreases)
    - A paid invoice is deleted
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Why might I see no data?">
    If you see "No data available", it means:
    - No invoices have been marked as paid yet
    - The revenue data hasn't been fetched from the database
    - There's a connection issue with the database
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Revenue data fetching

Revenue data is fetched from the database using the `fetchRevenue()` function:

```typescript
export async function fetchRevenue() {
  const data = await sql<Revenue>`SELECT * FROM revenue`;
  return data.rows;
}
```

<Warning>
  Revenue data fetching can be configured to prevent caching. This ensures you always see the most up-to-date financial information.
</Warning>

## Interpreting revenue trends

Use the revenue chart to identify important business patterns:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify growth trends">
    Look for upward trends across multiple months to confirm business growth
  </Step>
  <Step title="Spot seasonal patterns">
    Notice which months typically have higher or lower revenue
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare year-over-year">
    If you have more than 12 months of data, compare the same months across years
  </Step>
  <Step title="Investigate dips">
    When revenue drops, check if fewer invoices were paid or if invoice amounts decreased
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Compare the "Collected" card total with your revenue chart. The collected amount should equal the sum of all monthly revenue bars.
</Tip>
